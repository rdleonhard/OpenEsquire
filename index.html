Code is Law

The rules governing Ethereum transactions are encoded in the form of smart contracts, which are self-executing pieces of code. This allows for the creation of transparent and immutable contracts that can be automatically enforced. This means that the terms of a contract cannot be changed or altered once they have been agreed upon, ensuring that all parties involved in a transaction can have confidence in the fairness and reliability of the system.

The use of smart contracts also allows for the automation of complex transactions and business logic, making it possible to execute complex agreements without the need for intermediaries or other third parties. This can greatly reduce the costs and friction associated with traditional legal contracts, making it easier and more efficient to conduct business on the Ethereum network.

Note, however, that we do not use "code is law" is the sense that blockchain transactions are somehow above or beyond the reach of the law. We always advise clients to comply with all relevant laws, rules, regulations, and public policy considerations when operating their businesses.
